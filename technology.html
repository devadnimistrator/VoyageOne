magic happens

CMS? Workﬂow? Middleware? Check, check, check. Superpowers? Check.

A unique platform

VoyageOne built ECOM360 because nothing like it existed. ECOM360 is engineered to support numerous sellers and operate ecommerce presence in China across multiple marketplaces in a consolidated manner for each seller.

Your unfair advantage

Thanks to its integrated architecure, ECOM360 allows VoyageOne to operate with speed and effciency because it.

I Removes or reduces many labor-intensive task
I Reduces data redundancy and error
I Enforces best practices across the board.

ECOM360 consists of several modules, tightly integrated around a common database and user experience.

The best reasons and best way to go big

At the core of ECOM360 is the Content Management System (CMS). All SKUs and their associated attributes and content are there, in both original and translated versions (1). The CMS also identifies missing content and triggers Workflow Production System (2) tasks for that content to be developed.

The Translation and Localization System (TLS) identifies content that needs to be localized, automatically translates certain attributes according to a brand-specific dictionary (for instance, colors), schedules workflow tasks for human translations, and provides translators with the tools to ensure consistent, on-brand localization. (3)

The Workflow Production System (WPS) is used by both the CMS and the TLS to schedule human tasks like copywriting, translation, photography, and more on a large number of assets. It manages tasks, approvals, and resources (human and otherwise) based on capabilities (4).

Feeding into the CMS from sellers’ systems and then feeding from the CMS into the various marketplaces is ECOM360 Middleware Layer (EML). EML also handles product taxonomy and attribute mapping and has important message switching capabilities (5). The EML is the module to which sellers and marketplaces connect their own systems to send and receive products, orders, shipping information and more.

Thanks to the Merchandising Management System (MMS), merchants can manage which product is available to each marketplace, at what price, and orchestrate promotions and marketing campaigns (6).
